▊ BLINDSPOT
// Privacy Policy — Last updated March 23, 2026

We don't collect anything.

Short version

Blindspot runs entirely on your device. Your keywords stay on your device. We have no servers. We receive no data. There is nothing to sell, share, or lose in a breach.

What we collect

Nothing. Blindspot has no backend, no analytics, no crash reporting, no telemetry, and no third-party SDKs. The extension is pure JavaScript running locally in Safari.

Your data and where it lives

The keywords you add, your topic preferences, your settings, and your usage stats are stored exclusively in browser.storage.sync — Apple's iCloud-backed local storage for Safari extensions. This data syncs between your own devices via your iCloud account. It never leaves your Apple ecosystem and is never accessible to us.

  • Keywords you add — stored locally/iCloud only
  • Pro status — stored locally/iCloud only; verified via Apple's StoreKit
  • Stats (articles blocked count) — stored locally/iCloud only, never transmitted
  • Browsing activity — Blindspot reads page text to scan for keywords, but this data is processed in memory only and never stored or sent anywhere

In-App Purchase

The optional $0.99 Pro upgrade is processed entirely by Apple's App Store and StoreKit. We never see your payment information, Apple ID, or any transaction details. Apple's purchase privacy policy applies: apple.com/legal/privacy.

Permissions

Blindspot requests access to all websites so it can scan page content for your keywords. This permission is used solely to blur content matching your keyword list. No page content is ever stored or transmitted.

Children's Privacy

Blindspot does not collect data from anyone, including children under 13. The app is appropriate for all ages.

Changes

If we ever change this policy, we'll update the date above and note the change in the App Store release notes. Given that we collect nothing, there's not much to change.
